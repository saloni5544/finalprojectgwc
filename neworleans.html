On August 29, 2005, Hurricane Katrina struck the Gulf Coast affecting many coastal regions. This hurricane was known to have 127 mph winds and was categorized as a Category Three storm. According to the FEMA, Katrina was the “single most catastrophic events in US history”. Repairs cost over $108 billion, making it the most costliest hurricanes in US history. In New Orleans, over 80% of the city flooded. Today, people are still suffering from the aftermath Hurricane Katrina caused.
